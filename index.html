About Me

Hello, It's Me.

This blog is documentation of my personal, intellectual exploration into trauma studies. You may know who I am, in this day and age it is incredibly easy to find out the identity of an amateur, anonymous blogger like myself. I would appreciate it if you would please keep quiet about who I am. Why I am doing this: When I was seven years old, I disobeyed my mother by repeatedly running beyond the bounds she allowed me to play tag in with another girl. I was playing tag on the sidewalk of a public parking lot and I was only permitted to run in front of the glass of the karate studio while I waited for my sister’s class to be over. My mother became very angry with me that day and her anger was the only emotion I remember from her. As an adult, I now realize that my mother was genuinely frightened for my safety. When we got home, my mother brought me into the kitchen and explained to me what could happen to a little girl if she went with or was taken by a stranger. I gulped glass after glass of water as she explained how a stranger could put his penis inside me when I was not ready for it, and how it would hurt me. For the rest of my childhood, I did not run outside of my mother’s approved boundaries again. The explicit and implicit threat of sexual violence influenced and continues to influence my behavior and thoughts in my everyday life, in ways that I still am not fully aware of. I know this is true for many individuals, particularly women, throughout the world and I would like to do what I can to try to understand, speak out against, and to end the violence and pain. My background knowledge regarding trauma is primarily centered on sex crimes, but I will endeavor to broaden my depth of knowledge so that I can intelligently discuss many different types of trauma and violence.
